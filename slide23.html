Work Directed to Service

• Service Orientation: "Man's work should be directed to the service of God and of his fellow men."
• Beyond Self-Interest: Work is not merely for profit or self-advancement."
• Building a Better World: Work contributes to building a more just and humane society."
• Transcendent Purpose: Work has meaning beyond economic production."
"When men and women provide for themselves and their families in such a way as to be of service to the community as well, they can rightly look upon their work as a prolongation of the work of the Creator, a service to their fellow men, and their personal contribution to the fulfillment in history of the divine plan."

Catholic Business Ethics in Practice

Principle Traditional Business Model Catholic Social Teaching Model
Primary Purpose Maximize shareholder profit Serve common good while maintaining sustainability
View of Workers Human resources/labor costs Persons with inherent dignity and rights
Decision-Making Based primarily on financial metrics Considers impact on all stakeholders
Environmental Approach Comply with regulations, minimize costs Stewardship of creation, sustainability
Community Relations Public relations and strategic philanthropy Authentic solidarity and subsidiarity
Practical Example:
The Economy of Communion initiative, inspired by Catholic social teaching, includes over 800 businesses worldwide that commit to sharing profits in three parts: reinvestment in the business, direct aid to those in need, and formation of people in a "culture of giving." These businesses demonstrate how work can be directed toward service rather than solely profit.
