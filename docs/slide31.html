Marriage as Covenant of Love

• Covenant Relationship: "Marriage is a covenant of love between a man and a woman" (Gaudium et Spes, 48)
• Dual Purpose: "Ordered to the good of the spouses and the procreation and education of children" (Gaudium et Spes, 48)
• Total Self-Gift: Spouses give themselves to each other completely and exclusively
• Permanent Bond: "What God has joined together, let no one separate" (Matthew 19:6)
• Sacramental Sign: Marriage signifies and participates in Christ's love for the Church
"Authentic married love is caught up into divine love and is governed and enriched by Christ's redeeming power and the saving activity of the Church, so that this love may lead the spouses to God with powerful effect and and may aid and strengthen them in the sublime office of being a father or a mother." (Gaudium et Spes, 48)
The Three Goods of Marriage According to St. Augustine
Fidelity
Fides
Exclusive faithfulness between spouses. Mutual loyalty and trust that excludes all other romantic relationships. Commitment to honor and respect one another.
"Let marriage be held in honor by all, and let the marriage bed be kept undefiled." (Hebrews 13:4)
Children
Proles
Openness to procreation and commitment to raising children. Responsible parenthood that includes education in faith and values. Generosity in welcoming new life.
"Be fruitful and multiply." (Genesis 1:28)
Permanence
Sacramentum
Indissoluble bond that reflects Christ's faithful love for the Church. Lifelong commitment "until death do us part." Witness to God's unbreakable covenant.
"What God has joined together, let no one separate." (Matthew 19:6)
