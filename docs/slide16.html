Moral Agency and Freedom

• Moral Capacity: Man is a moral being, capable of choosing between good and evil (Gaudium et Spes, 9)
• Call to Virtue: Called to live a life of virtue and strive for holiness (Gaudium et Spes, 9)
• Authentic Freedom: Freedom is not license, but the capacity for responsible self-determination (Gaudium et Spes, 17)
• Conscience: The inner sanctuary where God's voice echoes in human hearts (Gaudium et Spes, 16)
• Dignity in Choice: "Only in freedom can man direct himself toward goodness" (Gaudium et Spes, 17)
"Authentic freedom is an exceptional sign of the divine image within man. For God has willed that man remain 'under the control of his own decisions,' so that he can seek his Creator spontaneously, and come freely to utter and blissful perfection through loyalty to Him." (Gaudium et Spes, 17)
Moral Decision-Making According to Catholic Teaching
1. RECOGNIZE THE SITUATION
Identify the moral question at stake and gather relevant facts
2. FORM CONSCIENCE
Consult Scripture, Church teaching, prayer, and wise counsel
3. APPLY MORAL PRINCIPLES
Consider natural law, human dignity, common good, and specific virtues
4. DISCERN INTENTIONS
Examine motives and intended ends of possible actions
5. MAKE DECISION & ACT
Choose freely in accordance with formed conscience and accept responsibility
