Structure of Gaudium et Spes

See-Judge-Act Methodology

  • Preface: Establishes solidarity with humanity (paragraphs 1-3).
  • Introduction: Analyzes the situation of humanity in the modern world (paragraphs 4-10) represents the “See” phase.
  • Part I: The Church and Man's Calling - doctrinal principles (paragraphs 11-45) represents the “Judge” phase.
  • Part II: Some Problems of Special Urgency - practical applications (paragraphs 46-93) represents the “Act” phase. It recognizes that human flourishing is not just spiritual but also encompasses social, economic, cultural, and political dimensions
  • Conclusion: Final exhortation and summary (paragraphs 91-93).
  • This methodology emphasizes that faith is not detached from reality but actively engages with it. This structure demonstrates the document's method of seeing (analysis), judging (principles), and acting (applications). From a theological perspective, this structure reflects the incarnational nature of Christianity. Just as God became incarnate in Jesus Christ, entering fully into human history, so too the Church is called to enter fully into the realities of the modern world.
