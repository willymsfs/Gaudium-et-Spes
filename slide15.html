The Social Nature of Humanity

• Inherently Social: "Man is a social being, and is called to live in community with others."
• Not Self-Sufficient: Humans cannot live or develop their qualities without relationships with others.
• Trinitarian Basis: Social nature reflects the communion of the Trinity.
• Progressive Circles: Social life begins in family and extends outward to wider communities.
• Mutual Development: "By his innermost nature man is a social being, and unless he relates himself to others he can neither live nor develop his potential."
Application in Catholic Social Teaching:
This understanding of human sociality forms the foundation for principles like solidarity, subsidiarity, and the common good. For example, Catholic healthcare institutions are structured to respect both individual dignity and social responsibility, providing care that addresses both personal needs and community health.
