Service to the Human Family

• Universal Service: "The Church is called to serve the human family, and to promote the common good."
• Following Christ's Example: Christ came "not to be served but to serve" (Matthew 20:28)."
• Practical Expressions: Service through education, healthcare, social services, and advocacy."
• Preferential Option: Special concern for the poor, vulnerable, and marginalized."
• Institutional Commitment: Service not just by individuals but through Church institutions."
"The Church, therefore, by virtue of the Gospel committed to her, proclaims the rights of man; she acknowledges and greatly esteems the dynamic movements of today by which these rights are everywhere fostered. Yet these movements must be penetrated by the spirit of the Gospel and protected against any kind of false autonomy."

Practical Example:
Catholic Relief Services (CRS) operates in over 100 countries, serving more than 130 million people annually regardless of race, religion, or nationality. Following the principles of Gaudium et Spes, CRS addresses immediate humanitarian needs while working for long-term development and social justice, exemplifying the Church's commitment to serving the entire human family.
