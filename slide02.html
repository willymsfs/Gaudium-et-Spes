Historical Context of Vatican II

• Timeline: Second Vatican Council (1962-1965)
• Pope John XXIII's Vision: "Aggiornamento" - updating the Church to address the modern world
• Gaudium et Spes: Final document of the Council, promulgated by Pope Paul VI on December 7, 1965 - 2,307 votes and 75 against it
• Significance: First time the Church addressed all humanity, not just Catholics. This shift the focus from internal Church matters to the broader human experience. Church’s mission as a sacrament of salvation for all
• Example: Previous encyclicals addressed "Venerable Brethren" while Gaudium et Spes addresses "the whole human family"
