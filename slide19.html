Human Equality and Rights

• Equal Dignity: "All men are equal in dignity, and are therefore entitled to the same rights and opportunities."
• Theological Basis: Equality derives from creation in God's image, common redemption in Christ, and common destiny.
• Against Discrimination: "Every type of discrimination, whether social or cultural, whether based on sex, race, color, social condition, language or religion, is to be overcome and eradicated as contrary to God's intent."
• Rights and Duties: Human rights correspond to human dignity and entail responsibilities.
"Since all men possess a rational soul and are created in God's likeness, since they have the same nature and origin, have been redeemed by Christ and enjoy the same divine calling and destiny, the basic equality of all must receive increasingly greater recognition."

Fundamental Human Rights in Catholic Teaching

Category Specific Rights Theological Basis
Life and Dignity Right to life, bodily integrity, adequate standard of living Created in God's image
Freedom Religious liberty, freedom of conscience, freedom of expression Human free will and moral agency
Social Family rights, education, work, fair wages, healthcare Social nature of humanity
Political Participation in public life, voting, legal protections Common good and subsidiarity
Contemporary Application:
This understanding of human equality has led the Church to advocate for the rights of migrants and refugees, condemn human trafficking, oppose racial discrimination, and promote equal access to healthcare, education, and economic opportunity. For example, Catholic Relief Services implements programs that uphold the dignity and rights of all people regardless of religion, ethnicity, or nationality.
